The legend of "Yi Sun Sin"
  • April, 28,1545, : He was born on April 28, 1545, in Geoncheon-dong, Hanseong, as the third son of Lee Jeong (李貞) and Chogye Byeon (草溪 卞氏)

  • 1576, (the 9th year of King Seonjo's reign), at the age of 32, he passed the civil service exam as the 4th person in the military service (丙科) [5] and received the Gwonji Training Center Bongsa (Training Center). He first took office as a volunteer trainee (not an official position).

  • May 23,1592, a Japanese fleet of 700 ships led by Konishi Yukinaga invaded Busanpo around 5 p.m., breaking out the Imjin War.Yi Sun-sin immediately sent a general message to the government and urgently summoned about 700 troops under his command and prepared them for defense.

  • August 14,1592, The Battle of Hansando was one of the three great battles of the Japanese invasions of Korea . It was a naval battle in which the Joseon navy under Yi Sun-sin defeated the Japanese army off the coast of Hansando.

  • March 12,1593, Yi Sun-sin's fleet advanced to Ungpo, Ungcheon-hyeon.However, in light of previous experience, the Japanese military judged that there was no chance of victory in a head-on battle, so they used an inducement strategy to lure the Joseon navy deep into the Japanese castle and defeat it.

  • April 11,1597,he was dismissed from his position as Commander-in-Chief, handed over his position to Won Gyun, and was transported to Hanseong and imprisoned on April 19th.

  • August 28, 1597, the Joseon fleet led by Won Gyun, who was appointed commander of the three provinces' naval forces , was ambushed by the Japanese at the Battle of Chilcheonryang and retreated to Chunwonpo. In order to resolve this, the Joseon Dynasty government again appointed Yi Sun-sin as the commander of the Samdo naval forces.

  • October 26, 1597, Yi Sun-sin, who received information about the movement of hundreds of Japanese ships, led a front line of 13 ships to battle in the Myeongnyang Strait.This is called the Battle of Myeongnyang, and with the victory of this naval battle, the Joseon Navy turned the tide of the Jeongyujaeran , which had put the country in crisis. Japan was in dire straits and attempted to reconcile by sending a bribe to the Ming Dynasty general, but Yi Sun-sin opposed this, and the following year, on August 18 of the lunar calendar, Toyotomi Hideyoshi (豐臣秀吉) died, and the Japanese army withdrew, leaving a will to withdraw just before his death.
